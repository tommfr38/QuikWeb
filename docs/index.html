Tiny app. Big shortcut.

Everything you need to search faster, and nothing you don't.

Global hotkey

Hit ⌥Space from anywhere — even inside full-screen apps. Fully rebindable in settings.

Your default browser

Results open right where you want them — Safari, Chrome, Arc, Firefox, whatever you use.

Launch at login

Flip one toggle and QuikWeb is always ready the moment you log in. No daemons to babysit.

Lives in the menu bar

No Dock icon, no clutter. A single status-bar glyph with exactly two options: Menu and Exit.

Light & dark themes

Match your mood with light or dark, plus a palette of accent colors. Switches instantly.

Any search engine

Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo — or a custom URL template for whatever you prefer.

Up and running in seconds

Self-contained installer. No dependencies — just macOS.

1

Download

Grab the installer .pkg and double-click it. It installs QuikWeb into Applications.

2

Open it

First time only, if macOS hesitates: right-click the .pkg → Open, then confirm.

3

Press ⌥Space

The search bar pops up. Type, hit Return, and you're off. That's it.

QuikWeb is open-source and ad-hoc signed (no paid Apple Developer account), which is why macOS asks you to confirm it the first time. It makes no network requests of its own — it only hands your query to your browser.
